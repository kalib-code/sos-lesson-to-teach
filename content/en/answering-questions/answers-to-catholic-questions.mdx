# Answers to Catholic Questions

### 1. To Those with Prejudice Towards the Bible

Catholics are urged to read the Bible, as the following official statements made by Catholic leaders demonstrate:

- "There is a challenge to read, study, and live the written Word of God. We, your bishops, have decided to proclaim 1989 as the National Bible Year!" — *Catholic Bishops Conference of the Philippines, Tagaytay City, Jan. 27, 1989*
- Pope Pius X declared, "The more we read the Gospel, the stronger our faith becomes."
- "Our desire for all the church's children is that they be saturated with the Bible, that they may arrive at the all-surpassing knowledge of Jesus Christ." — *Spiritus Paraclitus, Sept. 15, 1920*
- Pope Leo XIII said, "Ignorance of the Bible means ignorance of Christ."
- **Above all, Jesus, the Head of the Church said:**

  > Whoever belongs to God hears what God says. The reason you do not hear is that you do not belong to God. — John 8:47

### 2. On Mass

The Roman Catholic definition and view of Mass: it is the "unbloody sacrifice of the body and blood of Christ. The Mass is the same sacrifice as that of the Cross." There are 200,000 daily Masses worldwide. If Christ is offered again and again, as they claim, then the Scriptures below are not true!

> Unlike the other high priests, he does not need to offer sacrifices day after day, first for his own sins, and then for the sins of the people. He sacrificed for their sins once for all when he offered himself. — Hebrews 7:27

> The death he died, he died to sin once for all; but the life he lives, he lives to God. — Romans 6:10

> For Christ also suffered once for sins, the righteous for the unrighteous, to bring you to God. He was put to death in the body but made alive in the Spirit. — 1 Peter 3:18

> And by that will, we have been made holy through the sacrifice of the body of Jesus Christ once for all...but when this priest had offered for all time one sacrifice for sins, he sat down at the right hand of God. — Hebrews 10:10, 12

### 3. On Purgatory

According to the Roman Catholic teaching, it is a state in which those who die guilty of venial sins suffer for a time. But the Bible says: **no purgatory.**

**a. Saints did not expect to go there:**

> While they were stoning him, Stephen prayed, "Lord Jesus, receive my spirit." — Acts 7:59

> Then he said, "Jesus, remember me when you come into your kingdom." Jesus answered him, "Truly I tell you, today you will be with me in paradise." — Luke 23:42-43

**b. Christ bore all the suffering, punishment, and penalty for our sins. He died in our stead:** 1 Peter 2:21-24; Romans 5:8; 2 Corinthians 5:21; Colossians 2:13-14.

**c. Complete satisfaction for all sins (past, present, and future) has been made to God for us through Christ at Calvary:** Isaiah 53:10-12; John 1:29; 2 Corinthians 5:18-21; Hebrews 10:10, 14; Colossians 1:19-20; 1 John 3:5.

**d. God does not accept any other cleansing, purging, sacrifice, or atonement for sin besides Christ's shed blood at Calvary:** Hebrews 9:11-14, 22; Hebrews 9:26-28; Romans 3:24-25; Romans 5:8-9; Ephesians 1:7; Revelation 1:5.

> Without shedding of blood, no forgiveness. — Hebrews 9:22

**e. There is no second chance!** Luke 16:19-31; Hebrews 9:27.

### 4. The Pope

The Roman Catholic concept: the Pope is the Vicar of the Son of God, "Vicarius Filii Dei," or one who takes the place of Jesus on earth. The Pope has been called "Our Holy Father" and "Our Most Holy Lord" (Encyclical Letter: Pope Leo XIII; Pope Pius X). But the Bible says:

**a.** Jesus alone is Lord! Ephesians 4:5; Acts 10:36; Philippians 2:11.

**b.** Jesus is the Head of the Church! Ephesians 1:22, 4:15, 5:23; Colossians 1:18, 2:19.

**c.** Jesus said clearly that His Vicar is the Holy Spirit! John 14:16-17, 26; John 15:26; John 16:7-8, 12-15.

**d.** Peter was not Pope, just a fellow Elder (a title held by many in the church: 2 John 1:1; Acts 15:2, 4, 6, 22-23). Peter was not a Lord! 1 Peter 5:1-4.

**e.** Peter did not exercise authority as Pope. Examples: Acts 8:14 ("They were sent..."); Acts 15:1-21 where James' advice, not Peter's comments, is followed in the final decision made by the whole church!

**f.** The early church did not recognize singular papal authority! Acts 10:25-26; 16:4; 21:17-19, 25; Galatians 1:15-17, 2:6-9, 11-14.

### 5. On Mary

**a. Mary is not the Mother of God** — she was the mother of Jesus (not mother of God, for God has no mother. The Godhead is only composed of three Persons, and there is no Mother). John 2:1-3; Acts 1:14.

**b. Mary's perpetual virginity** could not be true in the context of Hebrew culture. Matthew 1:25; John 7:1-5 (with Psalm 69:9); Galatians 1:19; Matthew 12:46-50; 13:55-56; Mark 6:3. Jesus had brothers. These were not cousins of Jesus as Catholics would like us to believe. The Bible uses a different word for cousins (Colossians 4:10).

**c. Immaculate Conception** — teaches that Mary was conceived without sin. But see Romans 3:10-12, 23; 5:12; 1 John 1:8; Ecclesiastes 7:20; Luke 1:47; 2:22-24 (note that Mary had to offer a pair of doves, one of which was for a sin offering — Leviticus 12:8).

**d. Veneration of Mary.** If Mary was God, Jesus had the chance to endorse her veneration, but He did not. Luke 11:27-28. Calling her "blessed" is not ground for veneration (Luke 1:28, 42, 48-49; Judges 5:24), and there are plenty of blessed people in the Bible.

### 6. Mediator / Mediatrix

God alone can answer prayer because He has the characteristics and ability to answer prayer. He is omnipresent, omniscient, and omnipotent. Jesus is God and therefore can hear our prayers. Hebrews 7:25; Romans 8:34; John 14:13.

Not so with saints and Mary. If they were present everywhere (omnipresent), knew all things (omniscient), and had all power (omnipotent), then they must be gods! Since they are not, they cannot hear our prayers!

- **How many Gods? How many mediators?** 1 Timothy 2:5; Hebrews 12:24.
- **Who brings us to Jesus?** John 6:37, 44, 65.
- **Who brings us to the Father?** John 14:6; 1 John 2:1; Hebrews 2:18; 7:25.
- The story in John 2:1-11 never suggests Mary's power to intercede!

### 7. Celibacy

On celibacy for their priests/nuns, the Bible says:

- **Priests have wives.** Luke 1:5.
- **Not a sin to marry.** 1 Corinthians 7:25-28.
- **Peter was married.** Matthew 8:14-15.
- **Better to marry than to burn!** 1 Corinthians 7:8-9.
- **Church leaders are tested in their family.** 1 Timothy 3:1-5.
- **Celibacy is taught by demons.** 1 Timothy 4:1-3.
