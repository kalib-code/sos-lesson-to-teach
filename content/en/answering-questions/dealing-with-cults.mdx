# Dealing with Cults

### Marks of Cults

#### 1. Extra-Biblical Revelation

Claims for its authority rest on some revelation apart from the clear statements of the Word of God, which often comes in the form of a "divinely inspired leader." But the Word of God is the final and complete revelation (Hebrews 1:1-2; Revelation 22:18-19; Galatians 1:8-9).

#### 2. False Basis of Salvation

Cults primarily come against the Gospel of the grace of God. Every cult in the world preaches "another gospel" and is therefore cursed of God.

- **Salvation by Membership** — that there is no salvation outside the church, and when they say "church," they are referring to their own group.
- **Salvation by Works** — what one believes is of little consequence; what is important is what one does.
- **Salvation by Character** — one will find the road to peace and brotherhood through development of "moral values and spiritual insights."
- **Salvation by Working Out One's Own "Karma" or "Law Works"** — what one is now is a result of previous works, and what one will become is the result of his present works.

*Refutations: Acts 16:31; Romans 3:23-28; 4:4-5; Galatians 2:16; 3:10-11; Ephesians 2:8-9.*

#### 3. Salvation Not Settled

Insistence that one can never know he has eternal life while in this world. The follower lives in constant fear that he has not done enough, given enough, prayed enough, or worshiped enough to be sure of salvation. However, the New Testament promises are the opposite of this.

*1 Peter 1:3-6; Ephesians 1:13; Hebrews 6:19; 2 Timothy 1:12; 2 Corinthians 5:1; Philippians 1:23; 3:20-21; Colossians 1:13; 3:4; 1 Thessalonians 4:17; 2 Timothy 1:10.*

#### 4. Presumptuous Messianic Leadership

Elevating the person and words of a human leader to a messianic level which only Jesus Christ deserves. Hebrews 12:2; 4:14; 1 Timothy 2:5; Ephesians 1:22-23; Colossians 3:11; John 1:19-23; 3:27-30.

#### 5. Doctrinal Uncertainty

Cults continuously change their doctrine in order to adapt themselves to new situations, arguments, or whims of their leaders. But Scripture is clear that craftily changing doctrine is a cunning device used by those who prey upon the unwary.

*Refutations: Ephesians 4:14; Matthew 12:37; 2 Timothy 2:2; 3:7; 4:3-4; John 4:19-23; 11:9-10; Acts 20:20-21.*

#### 6. Claim of "Special Revelations"

Private individuals, usually the cult leader, make claims to specific discoveries. But the Christian faith is based on historical fact, and there is no discovery in the entire universe that anyone could possibly have that is superior to the discovery of salvation in Jesus Christ. There is no higher information, no better revelation, no deeper truth — nothing is greater than the knowledge of Christ.

#### 7. Defective Christology

The person of Christ as the Son of God is obscured, making salvation based on the saving work of Christ impossible. It is not a new tactic. It has been at work even during the times of the early church. Colossians 2:8-9, 18-19; John 5:23.

#### 8. Enslaving Organizational Structure

Cults bring their followers into psychological and spiritual slavery. The Word of God teaches that spiritual leadership is to be the leadership of example.

*Refutations: 1 Peter 5:3; Galatians 5:1, 13; 1 Corinthians 7:23; 2 Corinthians 3:17.*

#### 9. Financial Exploitation

Cultic groups imply that money contributed to the cause will buy privileges or gifts or powers. But the Word of God teaches that the Christian is never put under obligation to do, give, sacrifice, or expend himself in any way to become surer of his salvation. Romans 6:23; 2 Corinthians 8:9.

#### 10. Syncretism

Gathers together all the "best qualities" of various religious points of view into a new and acceptable faith. It synchronizes.

### Guidelines for Engaging with a Cult Adherent

1. **Guide the Conversation** — Ask thoughtful questions. Cult members often rely on memorized scripts and may struggle with unexpected dialogue.
2. **Avoid Arguments** — Stay respectful. Even if you disagree, acknowledge their right to their beliefs.
3. **Don't Mock** — Be compassionate. Their beliefs are deeply meaningful to them.
4. **Respect Their Founder** — Criticizing the cult's leader can create defensiveness and shut down communication.
5. **Use Scripture** — God's Word is powerful (Hebrews 4:12). Let it speak for itself.
6. **Share Your Testimony** — Personal stories of Christ's impact are hard to refute and deeply compelling.
7. **Lift Up Jesus** — Focus on Christ's identity and authority (Matthew 3:17; Philippians 2:9-11).
8. **Rely on the Holy Spirit** — Trust the Spirit to guide your words and actions (John 16:13-14).
9. **Know Their Beliefs** — Understand their doctrine and key verses to gently challenge their narrative.

## Mormonism

### Mormonism: A Brief Overview

#### 1. Founder — Joseph Smith Jr.

Born in 1805 in Vermont, Joseph Smith came from a poor farming family. His parents were involved in folk practices — his mother was known for fortune-telling, and his father claimed to have visions. Joseph and his father often searched for buried treasure. He had little formal education and learned to read and write later in life.

#### 2. His Vision

During a time of religious revival in the 1820s, Joseph claimed that at age 14 he saw a vision of God and Jesus. He said they told him not to join any existing churches because they had strayed from the true gospel. He believed he was chosen to restore the true church.

#### 3. Moroni and the Golden Plates

In 1823, Joseph said an angel named Moroni appeared to him and revealed the location of golden plates buried in a hill near his home. These plates, according to him, contained ancient writings that he later translated into what became the Book of Mormon.

#### 4. The Book of Mormon

Mormons believe this book is a divinely inspired record of ancient American civilizations and contains the fullness of the gospel (Doctrine and Covenants 20:9). It tells the story of two groups of people who migrated from the Old World to the Americas.

## Iglesia ni Cristo

### Iglesia ni Cristo (INC) — Overview

#### 1. Founder — Felix Manalo

Born on May 10, 1896, in Taguig, Rizal, into a Roman Catholic family. He explored various religions — including Catholicism, a mystical Mt. Banahaw group, Methodism, Presbyterianism, the Church of Christ, and Seventh-day Adventism — before founding INC.

#### 2. Development of INC

INC began with two congregations in Sta. Ana, Manila, and Tipas, Taguig. In 1922, amid accusations of immorality and leadership challenges, Manalo introduced the doctrine of the "Sugo" (God's messenger), claiming to fulfill Revelation 7:1-3 as the "angel from the east." This doctrine gave him supreme authority and helped solidify his leadership. The church expanded rapidly across Luzon and the Visayas during the 1920s and 1930s.

#### 3. Reasons for Growth

Several factors contributed to INC's rapid expansion:

- Active lay participation
- Strong centralized leadership
- Social and cultural shifts
- Effective financial management
- The influence of the *Pasugo* magazine
- Manalo's charismatic leadership

#### 4. Major Teachings of INC

- **About Felix Manalo** — INC teaches that Felix Manalo is the "fifth angel" or "angel from the east" mentioned in Revelation 7:2-3. They interpret "angel" here as a human messenger, not a heavenly being.
- **About the Scriptures** — INC affirms the infallibility of the Bible, but teaches that only their authorized ministers can correctly interpret it. Ordinary members or outsiders are discouraged from interpreting Scripture independently.
- **About God and Jesus Christ** — INC denies the Trinity, the deity of Christ, and His pre-existence. They teach that Jesus is only a man, though exalted by God.

  Scriptures they use:
  - *John 8:40* — "...a man who has told you the truth..."
  - *John 4:6* — Jesus was weary from His journey.
  - *Isaiah 40:28* — God does not grow tired or weary.

### Biblical Refutation

**Christ's Humanity** (affirmed in Scripture):

- *John 8:40* — Jesus refers to Himself as a man.
- *1 Timothy 2:5* — "...the man Christ Jesus."
- *Isaiah 53:3* — "...a man of sorrows..."
- *Acts 13:38* — Forgiveness of sins is preached through this man.

**Christ's Divinity** (clearly taught in Scripture):

- *John 1:1-3, 14* — "The Word was God... and the Word became flesh."
- *John 20:28-29* — Thomas calls Jesus "My Lord and my God."
- *Colossians 2:9* — "For in Christ all the fullness of the Deity lives in bodily form." *This is the clearest single verse affirming both Christ's humanity and divinity.*
