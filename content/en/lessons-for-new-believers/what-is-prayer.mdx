# What is Prayer

When you accepted Jesus as your Savior, God accepted you into His family. You are God's child. As God's child, you can now talk to your Father in Heaven through prayer.

### Definition

1. **Prayer is communication with God.** This means:

   - **Talking to God** — what a privilege for man to be able to talk with Him who has the highest honor, highest position, greatest power, King of Kings and Lord of Lords, Creator of the Universe!
   - **Dialogue** — God talks to you, you talk to God. Consequently, prayer involves listening. God listens; be sure you listen too!

2. **Prayer is a way of life.**

   - It is as normal and constant as our breathing. The Bible says we are to pray without ceasing.

     > Pray continually. — 1 Thessalonians 5:17

   - It comes from an attitude of dependence on Him — that apart from God we are nothing and can do nothing.

     > I am the vine; you are the branches. If a man remains in me and I in him, he will bear much fruit; apart from me you can do nothing. — John 15:5

### Preparation for Prayer

The believer must be cleansed from sin. A time of confession is most necessary to be an effective prayer person.

> ...the effective, fervent prayer of a righteous man avails much. — James 5:16

### Important Instructions for Prayer

#### 1. Address Prayer to the Father

Jesus addressed His prayer to the Father and He taught us to do the same.

> After Jesus said this, he looked toward heaven and prayed: "Father, the time has come. Glorify your Son, that your Son may glorify you." — John 17:1

> This, then, is how you should pray: "Our Father in heaven, hallowed be your name..." — Matthew 6:9

We address our prayer to God because He alone can hear and is able to answer prayers. He is not like the dumb idols of the pagans. *See also: Psalms 65:1-2; Psalms 115:1-7; Isaiah 44:9-19.*

#### 2. Pray in Jesus' Name

Jesus gave us the legal right to use His Name in prayer when we belong to Him.

> Very truly I tell you, my Father will give you whatever you ask in my name. — John 16:23

Remember, too, that we have no access to the Father by ourselves, or by any other name, but only through Jesus.

> Jesus answered, "I am the way and the truth and the life. No one comes to the Father except through me." — John 14:6
