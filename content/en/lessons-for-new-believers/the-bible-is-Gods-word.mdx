# The Bible is God's Word

### Introduction: Facts About the Bible

1. It is consistently the world's best seller.
2. Originally written in three languages — Hebrew, Aramaic, and Greek — the Bible is the book that has been translated into more languages than any other book.
3. It is the most persecuted book. Many tried to destroy it by burning or banning it, yet it still is the book that is most found in homes, hospitals, and other institutions.
4. It is one of the oldest books in the world, yet its message is applicable to modern times. It gives answers to the greatest questions that man asks through generations: *Where did I come from? Where am I going? Why am I here?*

These facts tell us that the Bible is not an ordinary book.

### The Bible — God's Word for Mankind

1. **The Bible is God's written revelation to man** through the process called *inspiration*. Many different people wrote the Bible by the inspiration of the Holy Spirit.

   > All Scripture is God-breathed and is useful for teaching, rebuking, correcting and training in righteousness. — 2 Timothy 3:16

2. **The Bible has one central theme.** Even though the Bible is made up of 66 books, it has one central theme: **God's redemption plan for mankind**. This theme is developed from the Old Testament to the New Testament. The Old Testament tells of God's work of raising up a nation through which Jesus, the Son of God, the Redeemer, will come forth. The New Testament tells about the birth, life, ministry, substitutionary death on the cross, and resurrection from the dead of Jesus Christ, the Son of God, the Redeemer.

3. **The greatest promise in the Bible:**

   > For God so loved the world that he gave his one and only Son, that whoever believes in him shall not perish but have eternal life. — John 3:16

### The Purpose for the Bible

1. **The main reason God gave us the Bible is for us to know the way of salvation.** The Bible teaches the way of salvation which is through faith in Christ Jesus.

   > ...you have known the Holy Scriptures, which are able to make you wise for salvation through faith in Christ Jesus. — 2 Timothy 3:15

   *See also: Ephesians 2:8; Romans 3:22-24; Romans 9:30-32; Acts 15:9; Genesis 15:6.*

2. **God's Word further teaches the believer how to live for God.** To be a Christian is not just having a religion. It's having a living relationship with the Holy God. Thus, a Christian ought to live a holy life like the God he believes in.

   > His divine power has given us everything we need for life and godliness through our knowledge of him who called us by his own glory and goodness. Through these he has given us his very great and precious promises, so that through them you may participate in the divine nature and escape the corruption in the world caused by evil desires. — 2 Peter 1:3-4

   > How can a young man keep his way pure? By living according to your word. I seek you with all my heart; do not let me stray from your commands. I have hidden your word in my heart that I might not sin against you. — Psalms 119:9-11
