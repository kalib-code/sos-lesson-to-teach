# Handling Persecution

Because you have now believed and accepted Jesus as your Savior, you are now on God's side. We are living in a world that has put God and His Word aside. So the Bible warns us to expect persecution if we live a godly life.

> In fact, everyone who wants to live a godly life in Christ Jesus will be persecuted. — 2 Timothy 3:12

If handled right, persecution is a blessed experience.

### 1. Persecution Brings Maturity

There are two things that make us mature Christians: the Word of God (1 Peter 2:2) and persecution or trials. If you are going to grow, you have to confront the world and endure persecution. That is a part of growing mature.

> Consider it pure joy, my brothers, whenever you face trials of many kinds, because you know that the testing of your faith develops perseverance. Perseverance must finish its work so that you may be mature and complete, not lacking anything. — James 1:2-4

> Blessed is the man who perseveres under trial, because when he has stood the test, he will receive the crown of life that God has promised to those who love him. — James 1:12

**Therefore, accept persecution as an essential part of growing.**

### 2. Persecution Brings Glory

People have persecuted Jesus ever since He was on earth. Today, the world still hates Him. When you receive persecution from the world, it's not because the world hates you — it's because the world hates Jesus. So persecution allows us the wonderful privilege of taking the arrows that Satan aims at Jesus. What a privilege!

> But rejoice that you participate in the sufferings of Christ, so that you may be overjoyed when his glory is revealed. If you are insulted because of the name of Christ, you are blessed, for the Spirit of glory and of God rests on you. — 1 Peter 4:13-14

> However, if you suffer as a Christian, do not be ashamed, but praise God that you bear that name. — 1 Peter 4:16

> If the world hates you, keep in mind that it hated me first. — John 15:18

> All men will hate you because of me, but he who stands firm to the end will be saved. — Mark 13:13

**Therefore, rejoice when you are persecuted.**

### 3. Persecution Brings Victory

Will persecution defeat us? God enables us by His grace. Persecution forces us to lean on Jesus. In our weakness His strength is made perfect. We experience that in His strength we can be victorious.

> My grace is sufficient for you, for my power is made perfect in weakness. Therefore I will boast all the more gladly about my weaknesses, so that Christ's power may rest on me. — 2 Corinthians 12:9

> Who shall separate us from the love of Christ? Shall trouble or hardship or persecution or famine or nakedness or danger or sword? As it is written: "For your sake we face death all day long; we are considered as sheep to be slaughtered." No, in all these things we are more than conquerors through him who loved us. — Romans 8:35-37

> That is why, for Christ's sake, I delight in weaknesses, in insults, in hardships, in persecutions, in difficulties. For when I am weak, then I am strong. — 2 Corinthians 12:10
